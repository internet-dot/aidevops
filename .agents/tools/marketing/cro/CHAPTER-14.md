# Chapter 14: Landing Page Teardowns

25 real-world teardowns across SaaS, e-commerce, lead gen, local business, and info products. Each identifies what works, what fails, and specific fixes with estimated impact.

## Universal CRO Patterns

These 8 patterns recur across nearly every teardown. Apply them as a checklist before diving into category-specific insights.

| # | Pattern | Problem it solves | Fix template |
|---|---------|-------------------|--------------|
| 1 | **Outcome-focused headlines** | Generic/vague value props ("Your digital HQ", "Easy scheduling ahead") | Replace feature description with specific measurable outcome: "Generate 3x More Qualified Leads" |
| 2 | **Pricing transparency** | Hidden pricing creates friction and unqualified leads | Show price range or "from $X/mo" near CTAs; use monthly framing for high-ticket items ($45/mo vs $1,095) |
| 3 | **Social proof at point of conversion** | Testimonials buried below fold, generic ("Great app!") | Place specific testimonials with outcomes near CTA: "Got $150K after my car accident — was offered $20K" |
| 4 | **Urgency/scarcity (authentic)** | No reason to act now vs later | Use real metrics: "10,000+ sold this month", "Currently accepting 2 new clients", stock indicators |
| 5 | **Reduce choice paralysis** | Too many CTAs, templates, products, or options | Clear CTA hierarchy (primary + subtle secondary); guided quizzes; curated "most popular" defaults |
| 6 | **Audience segmentation** | One generic page for all visitors | Separate landing pages per role/niche/use-case with specific messaging per audience |
| 7 | **Risk reversal** | Purchase anxiety, commitment fear | Free trials, money-back guarantees, "no credit card required", free shipping, easy returns |
| 8 | **Show before asking** | Users must commit before seeing value | Free tools, sample chapters, interactive demos, video walkthroughs, mini-courses |

## SaaS Teardowns (#1–5)

### #1: Slack (slack.com) — Free trial / contact sales

**Works:** Clear value prop, strong logo social proof (IBM, Intuit), dual CTAs for different buyers, video demo, fast loading (<2s), mobile-responsive.

**Fails:** "Your digital HQ" is vague to non-users; feature-focused copy instead of outcomes; no visible pricing; 3 competing CTAs create paradox of choice.

**Key fixes (est. +15–25% trial signups):**

- Headline → "Where work gets done: Collaborate 10x faster with your team"
- Add "From $0 to $12.50/user/month" near CTAs
- Single primary CTA "Try Free for 14 Days" with smaller "Or talk to sales →"
- Rewrite benefits as pain points solved: "Find any message instantly — no more digging through email"

### #2: Grammarly (grammarly.com) — Free signup

**Works:** Free tier prominent, real-time visual demo, "Trusted by 30 million", simple email-only signup, cross-device messaging.

**Fails:** Premium vs Free value unclear upfront; no specific outcomes; no user testimonials on homepage.

**Key fixes (est. +20–30% signups, +10–15% Premium conversions):**

- Add "Premium users catch 5x more errors" with tier comparison table
- Specific use-case headlines: "Write emails that get responses. Essays that get A's. Posts that get engagement."
- Add "Try Grammarly now" sample text box for instant demo before signup
- Real user story: "How Grammarly helped Sarah get promoted" with before/after

### #3: Notion (notion.so) — Free signup

**Works:** Sleek design, video demo, template showcase, free tier, beautiful UI screenshots, mobile app featured.

**Fails:** "All-in-one workspace" is vague — what does it replace? Too broad for first-time visitors; 50+ templates overwhelm; no quantified outcomes.

**Key fixes (est. +25–40% signups, better activation):**

- Audience-specific pages: `/for/students`, `/for/startups`, `/for/writers`
- Headline → "Replace Evernote, Trello, Google Docs, and Confluence with one tool"
- "New to Notion? Start with this 5-minute interactive tour"
- Add ROI: "Teams reduce tool sprawl by 70% and save $500/person/year"
- Show 5 template categories instead of 50 templates

### #4: Zoom Webinar (zoom.us/webinar) — Free trial

**Works:** Clear product differentiation from Meetings, feature comparison, "Up to 100,000 attendees", use cases, visible pricing tiers.

**Fails:** Generic headline; feature-heavy/benefit-light; no urgency; limited social proof; complex pricing with add-ons.

**Key fixes (est. +15–25% trial starts):**

- Headline → "Generate 3x More Qualified Leads from Your Webinars"
- Add customer success story: "How [Company] Generated 5,000 MQLs"
- Interactive ROI calculator: your attendees × conversion rate = leads, with vs without engagement features
- Simplify pricing to 3 tiers (500/1000/Custom), hide add-ons until checkout

### #5: HubSpot Marketing Hub (hubspot.com/products/marketing) — Free trial / demo

**Works:** Comprehensive features, integration ecosystem, free tier, strong customer logos, Academy/certification credibility.

**Fails:** Overwhelming for first-time visitors; feature-focused vs outcome-focused; pricing opacity; "Grow better" is vague; too many competing CTAs.

**Key fixes (est. +20–35% qualified leads):**

- Role-based pages: Marketing Directors (attribution/ROI), Content Marketers (blogging/SEO), Demand Gen (lead capture/conversion)
- Headline → "Generate 40% More Qualified Leads Without Increasing Budget"
- All-in cost calculator showing bundled pricing with common add-ons
- Single primary CTA "Start Free Trial" with subtle "Or see a personalized demo"
- 3–5 video testimonials with specific results

## E-Commerce Teardowns (#6–10)

### #6: Glossier Boy Brow (glossier.com/products/boy-brow) — Purchase

**Works:** Clean minimal design, high-quality multi-angle images, UGC photos, 4.3★ from 10K+ reviews, easy shade selector, ingredient transparency, mobile-optimized.

**Fails:** Limited info above fold; no urgency/scarcity indicators; shipping cost hidden until checkout; no cross-sells; no application video.

**Key fixes (est. +10–20% add-to-cart, +15–25% AOV):**

- Add "10,000+ sold this month" social proof urgency
- Show "Free shipping on orders $30+" below price
- Embedded 30-second application tutorial video
- Bundle upsell: "Boy Brow + Brow Flick $42 (save $5)"
- Move top 3 reviews with images above the fold

### #7: Allbirds Wool Runners (allbirds.com) — Purchase

**Works:** Sustainability messaging, "World's most comfortable shoe", merino wool innovation, size guide, free returns, UGC, color variations.

**Fails:** Vague comfort claim needs proof; no stock indicators; reviews not prominent; no competitor comparison; shipping time unclear.

**Key fixes (est. +15–25% conversion):**

- Quantify: "Rated 4.8/5 by 100K+ customers. 97% say most comfortable shoe they own."
- Stock indicators: "Only 3 left in your size" or "200+ sold today"
- Comparison table: Allbirds vs Traditional (sustainable materials ✓/✗, machine washable ✓/✗, odor-resistant ✓/✗)
- "Order in next 2 hours for delivery by Tuesday"
- Show "4.8★ from 100,000+ reviews" immediately below product name

### #8: Warby Parker (warbyparker.com/eyeglasses) — Home Try-On / Purchase

**Works:** Home Try-On (5 frames free), Virtual Try-On AR, "$95 including prescription lenses", filtering by shape/color/material, "Buy a pair, give a pair", free 30-day returns.

**Fails:** 100+ frames overwhelm; Home Try-On requires selecting 5 (commitment); prescription input confusing; no urgency.

**Key fixes (est. +20–30% Try-On signups, +10–15% purchases):**

- "Not sure which frames? Take our 2-minute quiz" → recommends 10 frames by face shape/style/lifestyle
- Simplify Try-On: "Start with 3" or pre-curated "Best sellers set" one-click
- "Order today, get 15% off first pair + free expedited shipping"
- "Don't have your prescription? Upload a photo of your current glasses"
- Video testimonials from customers who switched from traditional optical

### #9: Casper Mattress (casper.com) — Purchase

**Works:** 100-night trial + free returns, "As low as $X/month" financing, awards showcase, layer breakdown, thousands of reviews, free shipping, model comparison.

**Fails:** Original/Wave/Nova differences confusing; $1,000+ sticker shock; no urgency; no sleep test videos; only compares own models, not competitors.

**Key fixes (est. +15–25% add-to-cart, +10–15% purchases):**

- "Which Casper is right for you?" 1-minute quiz by sleep position/firmness/budget
- Lead with "$45/month" large, "$1,095 or pay monthly" smaller
- "Save $200 on any mattress this weekend only"
- 2-minute video comparing Original vs Wave vs Nova — who each suits
- Competitor comparison table: Casper vs Tempur-Pedic vs Purple (price, trial period, warranty)

### #10: Dollar Shave Club (dollarshaveclub.com) — Subscription signup

**Works:** "$3/month" prominent, starter set visual, savings vs retail, "Cancel anytime", premium positioning at low price, add-ons, testimonials.

**Fails:** Pricing bait-and-switch feel ($3/mo headline but starter $5, then $9/mo); complex customization friction; no visual of box contents; shipping frequency unclear.

**Key fixes (est. +20–30% signups, +10% AOV):**

- Honest pricing: "Starter Set: $5 (one-time). Then $9/month for refills."
- Video/carousel unboxing showing exact box contents
- 3 pre-configured bundles instead of separate customization: Essential $9, Premium $15, Ultimate $22
- Gift subscription option: "Give 3 months of smooth shaves"
- "Join 4 million members who never run out of razor blades"

## Lead Generation Teardowns (#11–15)

### #11: Neil Patel SEO Analyzer (neilpatel.com/seo-analyzer) — Email capture

**Works:** Free high-value tool, instant personalized report, authority brand, simple URL+email form, visual report preview, case studies.

**Fails:** Generic headline; privacy concerns (spam fear); no report preview without submitting; testimonials below fold.

**Key fixes (est. +15–25% email captures):**

- Headline → "Discover Exactly Why You're Not Ranking #1 on Google (Free 60-Second Analysis)"
- Privacy reassurance below email: "No spam, unsubscribe anytime. Used by 500,000+ marketers."
- "See example report" link with anonymized sample
- Social proof directly above form: "Join 500,000+ marketers"
- Exit-intent popup: "Wait! Get your free report + 10 SEO tips"

### #12: HubSpot Website Grader (website.grader.com) — Lead capture

**Works:** Instant report, actionable recommendations, brand trust, mobile-friendly, shareable results, just enter URL, upsell path to HubSpot tools.

**Fails:** No email required upfront (misses lead capture); generic scoring; surface-level report; no competitor comparison; no urgency.

**Key fixes (est. +30–40% email captures):**

- Show basic score free, gate full 50-point report behind email
- "Compare to competitors" — enter competitor URL to benchmark
- Specific fixes: "Your mobile score is 64/100. Fix these 3 things to reach 90: compress images, enable caching, minify CSS"
- "Upgrade to Premium Audit ($99) — 50% off for 24 hours"
- Gamification: "Your score: 67/100 (Better than 54% of sites). Retake in 30 days!"

### #13: Leadpages Templates (leadpages.com/templates) — Trial signup

**Works:** Visual preview, category filtering, conversion rates shown ("converts at 34%"), 14-day trial, drag-and-drop editor, mobile previews.

**Fails:** 200+ templates overwhelm; generic categories too broad; no guidance for specific needs; conversion rate claims unverified; no style filters.

**Key fixes (est. +20–30% trial signups):**

- Guided quiz: "What are you trying to accomplish?" → recommends 5 best templates
- Verified badges: "✓ Verified: 32% avg conversion (from 500+ users)"
- Default view: "Most popular this month" (10 templates), with "Browse all 200+" option
- Style filters: Minimal, Bold, Professional, Creative
- Industry collections: "Real Estate Agent Templates", "SaaS Startup Templates"

### #14: ConvertKit (convertkit.com/creators) — Email tool signup

**Works:** Creator-focused positioning, real success stories, free tier, visual email builder, migration service from competitors, strong testimonials, 14-day paid trial.

**Fails:** Generic "creator" term; pricing scales expensively; limited differentiation vs Mailchimp; feature-focused copy; no ROI calculator.

**Key fixes (est. +15–25% signups):**

- Niche pages: `/podcasters`, `/bloggers`, `/course-creators`, `/newsletters`
- Prominent "ConvertKit vs Mailchimp" comparison: "Built for creators, not corporate marketers"
- Revenue calculator: 5K subscribers × 2% conversion × $100 product = $10K/mo → improve to 4% = $20K/mo
- Transparent growth pricing chart: Free at 1K, $41/mo at 5K, $66/mo at 10K
- "Switch from Mailchimp in 1 click" with migration video

### #15: Calendly (calendly.com) — Scheduler signup

**Works:** Instant value (free account, schedule immediately), visual demo, integrations (Google Calendar, Zoom), use case variety, strong testimonials, mobile app.

**Fails:** "Easy scheduling ahead" is generic; many competitors; feature-focused; no urgency; tier differences unclear.

**Key fixes (est. +20–30% signups):**

- Headline → "Stop the Back-and-Forth: Schedule Meetings in Seconds, Not Days"
- Time-saved calculator: 10 hrs/mo × 12 = 120 hrs/yr, Calendly saves 80% = 96 hrs (2+ weeks)
- Visual before/after: "Without: 8 emails, 2 days. With: 1 link, instant booking"
- "Sign up this week: Get 2 months of Professional free (save $20)"
- Crystal-clear tier table: Basic (1 calendar), Essential (+reminders), Professional (+team scheduling)

## Local Business Teardowns (#16–18)

### #16: Local HVAC Company — Calls / form submissions

**Works:** Click-to-call prominent, service area map, Google/Yelp reviews, 24/7 emergency service, certifications (licensed/insured/bonded), before/after photos.

**Fails:** Generic stock photos; wall of text; no pricing (even ballpark); no online booking; 5+ second load time; not mobile-optimized.

**Key fixes (est. +30–50% calls and form submissions):**

- Real team photos with names: "Meet John, your lead technician — 15 years experience"
- Ballpark pricing: "Typical AC repair: $150–$400. Free diagnostic with any repair."
- Online booking calendar: "Book your appointment now — slots filling fast"
- 3 video testimonials from homeowners
- Speed optimization to <2s (53% of mobile users abandon after 3s)
- Mobile-first: large tap targets, sticky [Call Now] button at bottom (70% traffic is mobile for local services)

### #17: Local Personal Injury Law Firm — Free consultation

**Works:** Free consultation, "No fee unless you win", case results ("$2.3M settlement"), attorney bios, 24/7 availability, multiple contact methods.

**Fails:** Aggressive/salesy design; too many CTAs (call/text/chat/form); generic testimonials ("Great lawyer!"); no educational content; too many practice areas.

**Key fixes (est. +25–40% consultation requests):**

- Educational content first: "What to do immediately after a car accident (Free Guide)"
- Specific testimonials: "Got me $150K — was offered $20K by insurance. She fought and won." — Jane D.
- Single primary CTA [Schedule Free Consultation], [Call Now] only on mobile
- Niche specialization: "Car Accident Lawyers — We've won $50M+ for Houston drivers"
- Detailed case studies: "How we turned a $30K offer into a $400K settlement"

### #18: Local Restaurant — Online ordering / reservations

**Works:** Visible menu, appetizing food photos, integrated ordering, reservation system, clear location/hours, Yelp/Google reviews.

**Fails:** PDF menu (unreadable on mobile); delivery options unclear; unoptimized images (slow); no specials/offers; limited social proof.

**Key fixes (est. +20–35% online orders and reservations):**

- Responsive HTML menu with dietary filters (vegan, gluten-free)
- Clear delivery: "Order via DoorDash, Uber Eats, or Direct (save 15%)"
- Daily specials: "Today: Half-price appetizers 4–6pm" (updated daily)
- Embedded Instagram feed with "#YourRestaurantName to be featured"
- Loyalty program: "Every $100 spent = $10 credit" (captures email)

## Info Product & Course Teardowns (#19–25)

### #19: Online Course ($497) — "SEO Mastery Course"

**Works:** Video sales letter, detailed curriculum, instructor credentials, student testimonials, 30-day money-back guarantee, payment plan (3×$197).

**Fails:** 10,000+ word sales page too long; hype-heavy/salesy copy; no course preview; unclear time commitment; no community mentioned.

**Key fixes (est. +15–30% purchases):**

- Free 3-lesson preview to demonstrate teaching style
- Transparent expectations: "12 hrs video + 8 hrs exercises = 20 hrs. Complete in 4 weeks at 5 hrs/week."
- "Join 5,000+ students in our private Slack community"
- TL;DR version (2,000 words) vs full details (10,000 words) — let user choose
- Value framing: "DIY SEO: $0 + 200 hrs. Agency: $60K+/yr. This course: $497, learn in 20 hrs"

### #20: Membership Site ($25/mo) — "Freelance Writers Den"

**Works:** Community focus, low monthly price, relatable founder story, member spotlights, resource library, cancel anytime.

**Fails:** Monthly value unclear; no trial; limited testimonials; vague outcomes ("Grow your freelance business"); no annual option.

**Key fixes (est. +25–40% signups):**

- Detailed monthly value: "4 live coaching calls, 20+ new templates, exclusive job board, community forum. $500+ value for $25"
- 7-day free trial
- Diverse testimonials from different niches (tech writer, lifestyle blogger, copywriter)
- Specific outcome: "Members increase income by average $2,000/month within 6 months"
- Annual plan: "$250/year (save $50 — 2 months free)"

### #21: eBook ($47) — "The Ultimate Guide to Copywriting"

**Works:** Solves specific problem, author credentials, chapter breakdown, reader testimonials, money-back guarantee, instant delivery.

**Fails:** No preview chapter; low price raises quality doubts; no upsells; PDF only; no quantity social proof.

**Key fixes (est. +20–35% sales, +25% AOV):**

- Free Chapter 1 preview
- Price anchoring: "Copywriting course: $997. Hiring a copywriter: $5,000+. This book: $47 forever."
- Post-purchase upsell: "Add companion workbook for $19 (50% off)"
- Multi-format bundle: PDF + ePub + Audiobook for $67
- "Join 10,000+ copywriters who improved their skills"

### #22: Coaching Service ($2K/mo) — Discovery call booking

**Works:** Personalized approach, coach bio with results, client results ("$150K revenue increase in 6 months"), free discovery call, detailed success stories, clear process.

**Fails:** "Contact for pricing" is friction; "Entrepreneurs" too broad; availability unclear; no video; long-winded copy.

**Key fixes (est. +30–50% discovery call bookings):**

- Transparent pricing: "Investment: $2,000/month, 3-month minimum"
- Niche down: "For 6–7 figure e-commerce founders looking to scale to 8 figures"
- Scarcity: "Currently accepting 2 new clients for Q1"
- 2-minute video introduction of coach's approach
- Shorten to essentials: problem → solution → results → testimonials → CTA

### #23: Webinar Registration — "10X Your Email List in 90 Days"

**Works:** Specific outcome + timeframe, bullet-point takeaways, speaker credentials, countdown timer, simple name+email form.

**Fails:** Fake scarcity ("Only 100 spots" never fills); obviously a sales pitch; no social proof; replay availability unclear; no past-webinar testimonials.

**Key fixes (est. +25–40% registrations):**

- Authentic social proof: "435 people already registered" (not fake scarcity)
- Honest disclaimer: "This free webinar includes an offer for our paid course (optional)"
- "Can't make it live? Register anyway — replay within 24 hours"
- Past attendee testimonial: "Implemented one tip, got 500 new subscribers in a week!"
- Live registration feed: "John from Austin just registered"

### #24: SaaS Free Trial — Project Management Tool

**Works:** 14-day trial, no credit card required, feature overview, customer logos, video demo, integrations displayed.

**Fails:** "Manage projects better" is undifferentiated; 50 features overwhelm; no onboarding preview; no competitor comparison; no urgency.

**Key fixes (est. +20–35% trial starts):**

- Differentiate: "The only PM tool built specifically for remote teams (not retrofitted from office-first)"
- Show top 5 features only, link to "See all features →"
- Onboarding preview: "Import projects (5 min) → Create first board (2 min) → Invite team (1 min) = productive in <10 min"
- Side-by-side vs Asana, Trello, Monday highlighting remote-specific features
- "Start trial this week: Get 1-on-1 onboarding session ($200 value) free"

### #25: Fitness App Download — Home Workout App

**Works:** Free download, app preview video, 4.8★ from 50K reviews, before/after transformations, workout variety, no equipment needed.

**Fails:** Subscription cost hidden ("Free" but $14.99/mo required); generic testimonials; no trial period mentioned; no competitor comparison; no influencer endorsements.

**Key fixes (est. +30–50% downloads, +15–20% subscription conversion):**

- Honest pricing: "Download Free — 7-day trial, then $14.99/month"
- Specific transformation: "Lost 30 lbs in 90 days following the 6-week shred. No gym needed!" — Mike T. (with photo)
- Prominent "7-Day Free Trial — Cancel Anytime"
- Comparison table: This App ($14.99/mo, no equipment, 1000+ on-demand) vs Peloton ($44/mo, $1500 bike) vs Apple Fitness+ ($9.99/mo, Apple Watch required)
- Influencer endorsement: "As seen on [Fitness YouTuber]'s channel — 2M views"
